The Family of Electrical Techniques

TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation) is an electrical treatment which consists of short pulses which are Biphasic symmetrical or asymmetrical in shape. It affects motor and sensory nerves. It can be used to treat musculoskeletal pain, primary dysmenorrhea, chronic pain and intractable angina; it is also used during painful procedures, for post-operative pain and during labour.
Galvanic is continuous Unidirectional current. It flows in a single direction for at least one second. It can cause strong sensory stimulation, hyperaemia and electrotonus. It can be used to relieve pain and accelerate healing. It is commonly used in iontophoresis and stimulating contraction in denervated muscle.
Sensory and pain Nerves are affected by short pulses. Stimulating these using electricity is mainly used to alter pain perception and cortical activity, it can also be used to reduce spasticity. It is used to treat low back, neck, knee and shoulder pain as well as post-operative pain, during child-birth and labour, primary dysmenorrhea and pelvic floor dysfunction.
FES uses a sharp rising pulse to avoid accommodation in the muscle nerves. Electrical stimulation can be used to strength normal muscles and weakened muscles including reducing atrophy. Different amounts are used depending on the muscle condition.
Interferential therapy is the application of multiple out of phase currents with similar frequencies. It is used to treat pain, stimulate muscles, increase local blood flow and reduce oedema. It can cover a larger area then TENS and sometimes penetrates deeper.
Faradic or Interrupted direct current consists of monphasic squared shaped pulses. It can be used for motor stimulation and pain relief.
High Voltage Galvanic Stimulation(HGVS) consists of a Twinpulse waveform. It is used to relieve pain.
This technique is a slternating current delivered in rectangular bursts.
